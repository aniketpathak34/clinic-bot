A · Pure wordmark — Stripe / Substack / Linear

Wordmark only

No icon. The wordmark IS the logo. Confidence in pure typography. "Setu" highlighted in cyan to make the brand-distinctive part pop.

B · Linked squares — Linear / Vercel / Notion

Two squares, slight overlap

Two cyan rounded-squares slightly overlapping. The overlap creates a third darker zone — that's the "Setu" (bridge / connection) idea done as pure geometry. No metaphor. No clichés.

C · Orbit dot — Cursor / Vercel / Loom

Solid disc + thin orbit

A bold cyan disc with a thin orbiting ring and a single dot on the ring. Reads as "always-on, live, connected" without using any healthcare cliché. Iconic at any size.

D · MS monogram — Anthropic / Asana / Notion

Geometric MS letterform

Custom-built "MS" geometry. Sharp, ownable, scaleable. The white "M" anchors the brand; the cyan "S" carries the identity. Works everywhere — favicon, app icon, email signature, business card.

My recommendation

For a B2B SaaS targeting Indian medical practices in 2026, I'd pick Option B (Linked squares) or Option D (MS monogram). Both are sharp, modern, ownable, and work at every size. Option B is more abstract / "Linear" feel. Option D is a stronger brand letter you can grow into. Pure wordmark (A) is risky for a healthcare brand because patients pattern-match logos faster than typography.
